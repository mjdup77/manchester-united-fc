→ Read the briefing: Leicester City 2015/16
Two single-side A4 coach-facing cards on the most recent freely available season of Premier League event-level data.

  • Card A — Leicester's attacking corners. Two right-side outswinger routines surfaced via xG/corner ranking; freeze-frame composite at the instant of contact shows the marking-picture pattern (Fuchs → Huth at the front-post 6-yard band). Concrete defensive triggers a coach can read and act on.
  • Card B — Leicester's goal-kick build-up. 96% long; the right-shallow zone takes 47% of all GKs (87% first-contact won, 44% second-ball recovered); 3-action post-GK chains scored with a hand-rolled Karun Singh xT grid; Vardy as actor or recipient on 36% of all complete chains. Concrete press triggers — start the press at the landing zone, not at the keeper; the third action is where the chain dies; bias the right-back's positioning toward Vardy.

The brief lines up directly with what the JD describes as the day job: opponent profiling, structured performance review, raw event data without pre-built dashboards, executive-level reporting. The methodology is data-source-agnostic and ports directly to modern Opta / StatsBomb commercial feeds. Every figure is honest about what event data alone cannot see — flagged in the captions and in the methodology appendix.

The advice I most took to heart while building this came from Stephen Russell, Data Scientist at Manchester United, who once told me: "identify football-relevant problems, design a solution, and implement it in a way that is useful for decision making — prioritise those elements over technical complexity." I have tried to do exactly that. Every figure was decided on the basis of what a coach could act on, then the code was built backwards from there.
